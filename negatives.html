Negative Effects

Acetaminophen has a narrow therapeutic index. This means that the common dose is close to the overdose, making it a relatively dangerous substance if you accidentally take over the amount prescribed. Individuals who have overdosed on acetaminophen generally have no specific symptoms for the first 24 hours. Although anorexia, nausea, vomiting, and diaphoresis are common initially, these symptoms resolve after several hours. After resolution of these nonspecific symptoms, individuals tend to feel better, and may believe that the worst is over. If a toxic dose was absorbed, after this brief feeling of relative wellness, the individual develops overt hepatic failure. In massive overdoses, coma, severe liver damage, and metabolic acidosis may occur prior to hepatic failure.

Therapeutic Index - Ratio of toxicity to therapeutic dose (Higher is better), if something has a low therapeutic index, it should only be consumed in small doses.

Anorexia - An eating disorder that causes people to stress about their weight and how much they eat (they starve themselves)

Diaphoresis - Side effect of a lot of drugs, sweating a lot

Overt Hepatitic Failure - When mental illnesses show up in patients with liver disorders

Metabolic Acidosis - When there is too much acid in the bloodstream
