What is Acetaminophen?

Acetaminophen, also known as paracetamol, is a drug that can be used to cure many mild aches from muscle and headaches to sore throats. Acetaminophen belongs in the class of analgesics and antipyretics, which work by changing the way the body senses pain and cools it down. It comes in the form of tablets, capsules, liquid suspensions, and effervescence forms. Like NSAIDs, acetaminophen does not cause euphoria or altered moods, but differs because not enough is known about its mechanics to classify it as an NSAID.
