Fun Facts

In 2011, the FDA put limits on the amount of acetaminophen that could be found in pain meds. Currently, prescription pain meds can include no more than 325 mg of acetaminophen in each tablet or capsule, and existing prescription meds with more than 325 mg of acetaminophen must be removed from the market by January 2014.

Although most commonly found in tylenol, acetaminophen can also be found in products such as NyQuil, Vicodin, and Percocet.

It is recommended to not take more than one medicine that contains acetaminophen at a time. For example, your risk of liver damage goes up if you take a medicine that contains acetaminophen to treat a headache, and while that medicine is still working in your body, you take another medicine that contains acetaminophen to treat a cold.

It is recommended to not consume Acetaminophen while under the influence of drugs or alcohol.

The results for a study conducted by general medical clinics in Atlanta and Chicago showed that nearly a quarter of the patients were at risk of overdosing on an acetaminophen medication by exceeding the recommended dose of 4 grams (4,000 milligrams) in a 24-hour period.

More than 60 million Americans consume acetaminophen on a weekly basis.
